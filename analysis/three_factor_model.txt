Three-Factor Model: Corrected Regression (2026-07-07)
=====================================================

Model: missing% ~ -1.22 + 0.77*mod4 + 2.95*is_prime + 0.44*log(ring_pairs)

Data source: Verified D4-inequivalent counts from Flammenkamp RLE database (n=7-20)
Ring pairs: C(R,2) where R = exact number of distinct distance rings per n
Method: Weighted least squares (weights = D4_total / mean)

Results (CORRECTED, with exact ring counts):
--------------------------------------------
  Intercept:  -1.22  (baseline — extrapolation, not meaningful)
  mod4:        0.77  (modest direct effect)
  is_prime:    2.95  (prime n have ~3% higher missing-center rate)
  log(rp):     0.44  (small positive — counterintuitive, likely proxies for n)
  R-squared:  0.834  (adj. R² ≈ 0.78, 14 points, 4 params)

OLD Results (buggy missing-center detection):
---------------------------------------------
  Intercept:  16.09
  mod4:        0.19
  is_prime:    4.08
  log(rp):    -2.29
  R-squared:  0.620

Ring counts used (exact, verified):
  n=7: R=10, n=8: R=9, n=9: R=15, n=10: R=14, n=11: R=20, n=12: R=19,
  n=13: R=27, n=14: R=25, n=15: R=34, n=16: R=32, n=17: R=42, n=18: R=39,
  n=19: R=51, n=20: R=47

Changes from buggy model:
  - R² improved from 0.620 to 0.834 (+21.4 pct pts) from data correction alone
  - log(ring_pairs) coefficient flipped sign (-2.29 → +0.44): the strong
    negative effect was an artifact of incorrect missing-center detection.
    The positive sign is counterintuitive but small — likely collinearity
    with n (larger grids have more ring pairs AND more missing solutions).
  - Intercept dropped from 16.09 to -1.22: old model massively overestimated
    baseline rates
  - mod4 effect increased (0.19 → 0.77), is_prime decreased (4.08 → 2.95)
  - Dominant predictor is now primality (is_prime), not log(ring_pairs)

Interpretation:
  - 83% of variance explained by three simple factors
  - Prime n consistently have higher missing-center rates (~3% effect)
  - Ring-pair density has minimal direct effect with corrected data
  - The remaining unexplained variance likely involves:
    * Alternating parity patterns (even composites always 0%)
    * Subgrid structure (GG vs BB ring distributions)
    * Interaction effects (mod4 × primality)
  - Model is exploratory; 14 data points with 4 parameters → avoid overinterpretation
