## 0. Thesis

The project's real subject is no longer "No-Three-In-Line" as an isolated
puzzle, but a **theory of symmetry-induced rigidity in extremal lattice
point configurations**, with NTIL as the experimental testbed.  Within that
theory, rigid structure appears in **two mathematically distinct layers**:

| Layer | Name | Order | Statement | Status |
|-------|------|-------|-----------|--------|
| 1 | **Linear rigidity** (FDR) | 1st-order (linear Sidon) | symmetry ⇒ arithmetic Sidon law on the fundamental domain | **THEOREM** (`fdr_theorem.md`) |
| 2 | **Geometric rigidity** (R8) | 2nd-order (quadratic) | NTIL ⇔ quadratic `(X)∧(S)` CSP on an m-subset | **THEOREM** (`r8_proof.md`) |

The two are linked by a strict inclusion: Layer 1 is *necessary* but *not
sufficient*; Layer 2 is *exact*.  The missing step between them is the
**Quadratic Gap** (R7): cross-quadrant collinearity is an irreducible
quadratic condition that no finite family of linear Sidon forms can capture.

## 1. Layer 1 — Linear Rigidity (Fundamental Domain Rigidity, FDR)

**Statement (Theorem FDR, `fdr_theorem.md`).**  Let `C` be a `2n`-point NTIL
extremal configuration, `G = Stab(C) ≤ D₄`.  If `G` *preserves the slope-±1
line family* (i.e. `G` contains no orthogonal reflection `g₄`/`g₅`, so `G` is
generated by rotations and/or diagonal reflections), then on the natural
fundamental domain `F_G` the configuration satisfies the a−b Sidon law
`count(d)+count(−d) ≤ 2`.

**Proof chain (geometric, not empirical):**
```
symmetry G ≤ D₄
   → orbit decomposition  C = ⊔ G·p_i
   → natural fundamental domain F_G  (one rep per orbit)
   → Lemma C2: G preserving slope±1 lines  ⇒  F_G has ≤ 1 point per slope±1 line
   → a−b = −2(x−y)  ⇒  each a−b value occurs ≤ 1, and the d↔−d pairing
      (from 180° rotation / diagonal reflection) gives count(d)+count(−d) ≤ 2
```
The boundary case is precise: **ort1** (orthogonal reflection) *swaps* the
slope+1 and slope−1 families, so no d↔−d pairing exists and the Sidon law
fails (empirically 0%); **iden** (trivial group) has no fundamental-domain
reduction and fails too.  FDR is therefore *not* "symmetry ⇒ Sidon" but
"*slope-line-preserving* symmetry ⇒ Sidon" — a sharply characterised theorem.

**Role:** Layer 1 explains *why* Sidon structure appears in symmetric
solutions (geometry forces difference-structure uniqueness) and supplies a
fast, necessary linear filter.  It is the upper bound of the rigidity
hierarchy.

## 2. The Gap — R7 (Quadratic Gap)

**Statement (Theorem R7, `quadratic_gap_theorem.md`).**  Cross-quadrant
three-point collinearity is an *irreducible quadratic* condition on the cell
variables `(α,β)`.  No finite set of linear Sidon forms (however many) can
capture it.  Consequently FDR (linear) is necessary but **not sufficient** for
rot4 NTIL: the set of Sidon-satisfying pairings is vastly larger than the set
of NTIL solutions.

**Algebraic witness:** for rotation pattern `(r₁,r₂,r₃)=(0,1,2)` the C4-lifted
collinearity determinant expands to a strict quadratic form in the
`(α,β)` variables (products `αᵢαⱼ, βᵢβⱼ, αᵢβⱼ`).  Linear Sidon covers only the
linear part `|c·a + d·b| ≤ 2`; the quadratic part is invisible.  This is why
all 55+ linear-Sidon searches for m=37 failed — they searched a *linear*
space where the obstacle is *quadratic*.

**Role:** R7 is the bridge that forces us down to Layer 2.  It converts
"FDR is nice but incomplete" into a precise statement about *what is missing*
(quadratic order).

## 3. Layer 2 — Geometric Rigidity (R8)

**Statement (Theorem R8, `r8_proof.md`).**  A rot4 (C₄-symmetric) `2n`-point
NTIL configuration exists **if and only if** an m-subset of the m×m
fundamental quadrant (one C4-orbit representative per cell) satisfies the
quadratic `(X)∧(S)` collinearity-determinant system:
- **(X)** for every triple of distinct cells and every C4 board-rotation class
  of rotation triples, a 3×3 collinearity determinant ≠ 0;
- **(S)** for every two images of one cell plus one image of another, the
  corresponding determinant ≠ 0.

The proof partitions any collinear triple by source orbit into exactly three
cases — (A) three distinct orbits (X covers), (B) two images of one orbit +
one of another (S covers), (C) three images of one orbit (a C4 orbit has no
three collinear points — geometrically impossible).  Cases A,B are ruled out
by (X),(S); C never occurs.  Hence `(X)∧(S) ⇔ no-three-in-line`.

**Variable correction (2026-07-12):** the m cells are an **m-subset** of the
m×m quadrant, *not* a permutation matrix (rows/cols may repeat — verified on
the full known-solution corpus).  The constraint count for m=37 is **140,304**
quadratic inequalities over 1,369 binary selection variables
(`r8_minimal_csp.md`); the system is overwhelmingly over-determined.
Equivalently, as an *exact* CP-SAT instance it expands to **1,264,378**
per-line weighted at-most-2 constraints over the 1,369 position-selection bits
(`cpsat_encoding.md`); the m=37 attack now runs there.

**Role:** Layer 2 is the *exact* characterisation.  FDR is a linear feature
implied by `(X)∧(S)` and redundant for R8's equivalence.  Layer 2 is where the
m=37 existence question actually lives.

## 4. The hierarchy

```
   symmetry  G ≤ D₄
        │   orbit decomposition → natural fundamental domain F_G
        ▼
   ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
   │ LAYER 1 — LINEAR RIGIDITY (FDR, THEOREM)             │
   │   slope±1-line preservation ⇒ a−b Sidon  [necessary] │
   └─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
        │   R7: linear insufficient; cross-quadrant
        │       collinearity is QUADRATIC (irreducible)
        ▼
   ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
   │ LAYER 2 — GEOMETRIC RIGIDITY (R8, THEOREM)           │
   │   (X)∧(S) quadratic CSP on the m-subset  [⇔ NTIL]    │
   └─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
        │   R6: the (X+S) system is 0-dimensional
        │       (over-determined ⇒ each cell forced)
        ▼
   m = 37 existence  ⇔  satisfiability of the Layer-2 CSP   [OPEN]
```

**Conceptual reading.**  Layer 1 is the *first-order projection* of the
rigidity condition along the symmetry axis: it captures the arithmetic
structure that symmetry *forces*, but it projects away the geometric
(cross-quadrant) interactions.  Layer 2 restores the full second-order
geometry.  Both are "symmetry-induced rigidity", but at different orders — a
clean two-tier structure reminiscent of additive vs. incidence structure in
modern combinatorics.

## 5. A third, speculative layer — structural classification (container method)

The **container method** (danger hypergraph `Hₙ`: vertices = lattice points,
edges = collinear triples; `2n` configurations = independent sets) gives a
hard foundation: codegree is exactly `n−2`, so all `2n` configurations lie in
≤ `exp(O(n))` high-minimum-degree containers.

> **Caution (per external review).**  This does *not* yet prove that contained
> configurations are symmetric or Sidon.  Container concentration is a
> *structural-classification framework*, not a rigidity proof.  The open bridge
> is: *does a high-minimum-degree container force a non-trivial symmetry G
> (hence bring the configuration under FDR / Layer 1)?*  Until that is shown,
> the container viewpoint is a **candidate Layer 0** (concentration), not a
> proved rigidity layer.

## 6. Outlook

1. **Linear → geometric completion.**  Layer 1 (FDR) and Layer 2 (R8) are both
   theorems; the gap (R7) is a theorem.  The two-layer structure is the
   project's backbone.
2. **m=37.**  Entirely a Layer-2 question (quadratic CSP satisfiability).
   Structural scalings (`m37_satisfiability_window.md`) give indirect evidence
   *for* existence (no phase transition at m=36→37, stable source-ratio ~0.26,
   flat constraint density).  The correct-space greedy solver
   (`constraint_prop_solver.py`, Stage M) cannot complete (dies at depth 25 ≪ 37)
   — exactly what the 0-dimensional global-rigidity picture (R6) predicts.  The
   prescribed next step is a **clause-learning CP-SAT attack in the exact
   per-line at-most-2 space** (`cpsat_encoding.md`, `cpsat_m37.py`): validated
   sound (m=3..15 discovery → full `(X)+(S)` check passes) and reachable
   (m=36 instance admits its cached solution).  OR-Tools attack on m=37 launched
   2026-07-13 00:14, **terminated by user ~00:15** (no solution found within
   budget; 3.8 GB peak).  The encoding remains the right tool for a future
   larger-budget run.  **OPEN.**
3. **Extremal-config taxonomy.**  Beyond symmetric (Layer-1/2) families, the
   abandoned SE conjecture leaves a larger question: how many *structure types*
   do extremal configurations have (symmetric / asymmetric / sporadic)?  This is
   the natural next frontier once the symmetric rigidity is fully mapped.

4. **Costas cross-application (NEW 2026-07-13).**  The two-layer / FDR symmetry
   lens transfers to the Costas array problem — the *same* D₄ lattice, classified
   from the complementary side ("which subgroups *can occur*").  Result: a Costas
   array admits exactly **6** D₄ symmetry types; `H`/`V` reflection is impossible
   (the FDR `ort1` boundary lifted to permutation-matrix structure); full D₄ is
   impossible; `C4` requires `n ≡ 0,1 (mod 4)` (`costas_symmetry_theorem.md`,
   theorems C1–C5).  Empirically (scan to n=13) `D4(full)=0` everywhere and
   `C2=C4=D2=0` — raising the open question of whether *rotational* Costas
   symmetry exists at all.  This narrows any symmetric witness at the open orders
   32/33 to the six-type list, and `cpsat_m37.py` is a direct template for the
   exact distinct-displacement CP-SAT attack on 32/33.
